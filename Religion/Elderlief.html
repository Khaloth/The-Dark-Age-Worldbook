The Myth

It is well-known that the Universe runs in cycles. There is the time of Dawn, of Ripening, of Blooming, of Decay, and of Death.

Much like a man, so do the stars, the planets, the gods die and regenerate. Following each Deathcycle, the Worldsbane flies over the world and burns it to ashes so that nothing remains and with the world, so do the Gods perish in the flames.

Alas, not all is lost, for in the deepest darkness of the world move the restless Shadows of the Elder Gods. Ancient beings which predate the first cycle. Being so old, they have seen the birth and death of countless worlds, and uncounted generations of Men's Gods.

They laugh as they watch the world burn and, just as it seems that all is to be destroyed, they save a man of every race to serve them eternally. They never quite manage to hold onto absolute power for long, however. Thousands, or millions of years may pass but they always, in the end, fall into petty squabble and loosen their grip. It is at this time that the New Gods, risen from the ashes of the Old Gods, snatch the Urfolk and convert them.

A great battle ensues between the New Gods and the Elder Gods. The result is never pre-decided. If the Elder Gods win, they take the Urfolk back and all goes on as usual until the New Gods are, once more, risen from the ashes of the slain to repeat their forerunners' deeds.

This cycle goes on util, eventually, the Elder Gods are defeated and through the New Gods come to rule the Universe once more.

To commemorate their victory over the Eldergods, the Gods plant a tree called Yggdrasil, Verdensooth or simply Worldtree on whose extensive boughs they build the over- and underwolrd as well as the world of men.

In the beginning were born the Gods Wodarr and Locken of the spilt blood of the fathergods.

The Gods

There are in total 34 gods divided into 8 Holy Rings of influence.
The Gods in their natural form have no face and hide the fact behind silver masks (men) and spider-silken veils (women). In visions to holy men, they appear to have the face that most suits them at the time; a beautiful young maiden, an aged elder-woman, a fierce, battle-scarred warrior.

The Gods are incapable of reducing their thoughts and speech to the level of deathlings. The simplest things they manage to formulate are barely intelligeble riddles for the ears of the living. One way to communicate with the living are rhymed constructs, which occupy sufficient amount of their intellect to reduce the complexity of their words most efficiently.

The Ring of Souls

The ring of gods who rule over the fluctuations of fortune and misfortune in the world and cater to the weave of fates of Men. They are the ones who manage the smooth and proper running of history and who settle disputes among the Gods. While they may not grant a victor-to-be the physical prowess to win a battle, they may grant him an advantage through manipulation of and direct command to the other gods or directly through a change of condition, such like snapping of the foe's blade or a stray arrow hitting him in the neck.

While the other Gods do have a measure of autonomy and fate generally is not immutable, their power grows proportionately to the measure to which the world had strayed from their vision of the future, eventually granting them the necessary might and authority to right a skewed version of history. For example, a hero might escape a foretold death through wit and help of other deities, but he is prevented from establishing a mighty dynasty which would rule over the surrounding land by rendering him heirless.

Creation

In the beginning there were two blossom-less roots: the root of poison and the root of fire. And these two roots merged and intertwined they grew into a single tree - the Verdensooth
The combination of fire and poison begot blood and blood begot life. The reason men die is because their blood contains the primaeval poison that kills them over time.

Duality of Men

Men have the inherent duality of their creation embedded into them. While the other races are made of usually a single substance characterising their racial behaviour, men are made of two. This duality is a double-edged sword, for it means men can both make to unmake and unmake to make. While fire may destroy forests and kill thousand in a single onslaught of destructive rage, it may also drive off danger in the night and provide men with warmth and clarity. And while in large doses poison kills, in small it often is a remedy. The fire and poison of which men are made can be used both for good and evil. This differentiates them from for example devils and daemons who are made of cold fire and true poison neither of which can be used for good.

Elves, dwarves, and other such creatures have more life than poison in their veins
Daemons, devils and so on, only poison
Men - half/half

The Seven Years of Winter

When the apocryphal Curst King served his rival his son after inviting them to feast in his halls, the Gods took a great offence and as punishment cast seven years of winter on the king and men.
