Darkspeech

Created Sunday 12 June 2022

Derived from Blackspeech; a hardly-recognisable super-simplification of Blackspeech.

IDEAS:
12 grammar-cases - all expressed through in-fixes? - the stem of the infixes changes based on one of the four moods: active dominant (the doer outranks the receiver), active dominated (the doer is outranked by the receiver)
5 genders based on vulnerability i.e. frail/defenceless, slightly resistant, resistant, sturdy, warrior

GRAMMAR


NOUNS

Each noun's form contains the following grammatical information:

  • Gender
  • Number
  • Declention Class
  • Animacity (being animate or inanimate)

GENDER

Every noun is assigned one of five grammatical genders based on its agreed or perceived vulnerability:

  • Frail / Defenceless
  • Easily breakable / Prey
  • Moderately sturdy / Threat
  • Sturdy / Dangerous
  • Unbreakable / Untouchable

Observe that when applied to inanimate objects, their sturdiness is measured, whereas if applied to animate beings their vunerability to physical attack is perceived instead. Moreover, the gender of a noun may change depending on the developing situation. For example a man who is perceived as a weak opponent might be apriori assigned the second gender and aposteriori a different one following an encounter. Therefore, the gender of a noun is more similar to true adjectives than in other languages; they nevertheless perform grammatical functions and therefore cannot be classified as such.

Partitive: Used similarly to the acccusative case except the accusative case only pertains to objects in their entirety whereas the partitive case only refers to a part of the object. For example:
- accusative: I'll burn down the castle (the castle will be reduced to rubble)
- partitive: I'll bring the castle down with catapults (implying only the walls will be brought down)

NUMBER

The number is indicated by one of the five following prefixes:

Singular True Singular Dual Trial Plural Superplural
- kal ral rwal rg' reeg

Observe that true singular is used when the described creature (always used of living things) is far removed from the aid of others. The superplural is used for very large quantities, fallen leaves in autumn, fighters in a large army etc.

ANIMACITY

Objects may either be animate, mortally inanimate, or essentially inanimate, as indicated by one of the two following infixes:

Animate Inanimate, Mortally Inanimate, Essentially
gh xh -

  • Mood
  • Speed
  • Violence-level
