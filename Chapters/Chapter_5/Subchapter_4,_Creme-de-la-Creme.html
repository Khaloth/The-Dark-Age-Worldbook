Subchapter 4, Creme-de-la-Creme

Created Wednesday 21 December 2022

The party arrives at Sir Hawk's house. A thunderstorm rages in the sky. As they enter, they oversee a domestic argument between Sir Hawk and his wife Mildred, who has been suffering from schizophrenic bouts since the War. The scene's purpose is to cast suspicion on Sir Hawk, but unjustly.
Mildred can be seen raising her fist at her husband and screaming:
IT'S GOT TO STOP. YOU'RE KILLING ME HAWK. STOP IT. STOOOP IIIT!
She notices the party. She points her finger at them and screams
DON'T LISTEN TO THEM. YE'VE GOT TO LISTEN TO ME
Hawk tries to wrestle her away, but she approaches the party and, wild-eyed, says
THEY'RE ALL LIARS. SO MANY DEAD. TAKEN AT THE FLOOD. HIS WORDS MERE NOISE MADE AT THE POISON-SMEARED LIPS!
She breaks
'SBLOODY FILTH!
And she runs away, followed by a servingman to her room.

A maid standing in the doorway stupidly, perhaps provocatively asks 'Is her ladyship alright?'

Sir Hawk apologises for the scene and explains that Mildred has been 'unlike herself lately'.

He asks them to wait in the parlour while the food is being cooked and says he's going into the kitchen to tell the cooks to hurry. He is instead coming upstairs to calm his wife down. The characters get to discuss their plans and snoop through various documents lying about in the parlour.

The party is spied on (clumsily) by Wendy, a little servantgirl. If entertained, she is willing to talk about Sir Hawk's private life.

Eventually, they are summoned for dinner. The storm worsens. Sir Hawk comments on the storm nervously before shifting the topic to the various art objects he has gathered over the years and probes the characters to tell him what historical or mythical scenes they depict. If they impress him, he will offer to gift them an unhanged piece that he owns, as additional thanks for their efforts at the Freyshrine.

The dinner is interrupted by Lady Gunnhild and her servants. She has armed guards burst into the room (to scare Hawk) before making her entrance. She comes to complain to Hawk about the industry's underperformance and makes known to him she is willing to have him replaced.

This puts Sir Hawk in a sorry mood. The storm intesifies. Hawk offers the group stay the night.
