Subchapter 3, Welcome to Crystalthorp

Created Sunday 20 November 2022

The Woods

The elves who had murdered the wagonners scatter. Before he leaves, Laminus turns around to the party and speaks to Malbor:
Bene fecisti, fratre generis. Memento fides ubi tua. Erat fas facu.
(Thou'st done well, race-brother. Remember where your loyalties lie. It was the right thing to do.)

The adventurers are left alone in the forest. Time is running short and it will be evening in roughly two or three hours.

As they advance down the road, they learn that one of the dying men is not dead. He is a merchant coming home to see his wife and a newborn son. He begs the adventurers to give a holy relic - a bone fragment of Mannig the Treeborn - his wife, Layn, so that she could pass it onto him. Then he dies.

Along the way, Aags raises the point that Malborn seemed to have enjoyed himself killing Old Lizzie and ponders what will happen with her son.
'Yeah, I hope that guy kills himself. With a bit of luck, his whore-mama's dying will make him go swing off some tall tree.

The Adventurers have about a two-league journey before them through the woods to Crystalthorp. Along the way, they find Hugue the Woodsman's house.

If the party is to leave without much interaction, Aags will urge them to ask which way to Will's house.

Willstead

The group is heartedly welcomed by the Willstead folk.

Arrival

The Preystalker is seen peering through the window observing the group. The Will family does not see him. He vanished before he can be confronted.

The Morning After

The willstead folk arise before sunset so that by the time the party wakes, Will has already gone with Ashley to the fields to continue with the harvest.
The party is awoken by Demmy who was tasked with arousing the group by his mother. She chides him harshly for being ungentle with the group.

She spanks him and throws outside. She apologises to the group and bids them eat their breakfast.

She asks the group about their life-stories. She reveals her husband's lifestory if given a chance.

There is a knock on the door. A neighbour-woman, Thrist, enters. She announces, breathless, that there has been another killing. Across Willstead fields near the woods the son of the local miller has been dismembered and the trees had been adorned with his guts. The doing of the Preystalker.

She also says that Hawk, the Guildmaster, has come to the village. She is unsure what to make of it. Has he come because of the killing?

When it comes to the group, she tells Osthrith 'I didn't know your folk was THIS MUCH desperate for coin'.
