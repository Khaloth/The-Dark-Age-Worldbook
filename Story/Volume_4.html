Synopsis

High Divine Waldemar was born on the remote Island of Halunnsore into the Order of Duskwatch. Since boyhood, Waldemar had a fascination with the Manifest Destiny Prophecy that Ashenwyrm should be supreme amongst all of the Northern Nations. Towards the end of his stripling years, he became rebellious. The very purpose of his Order was to await the coming of the Golden Dove bearing the Oakapple of Forgiveness, inviting Men to return home to Wereheim, but he himself could not believe this would ever happen. He had a falling-out with the other memebers of the Order and he was expelled. In rage, he set fire to their temple (called Wyrdsbarrow), trapping his masters in the burning building by barring their doors.

Trapped within the burning temple, Gauldoth, a fellow-acolyte, nearly lost his life during the fire. 'Naked and with burns that would soon claim my life, I opened a scroll that was far beyond my power. I was dying. I could feel my scorched lungs slowing, unable to take another breath. So, I read the words on the ancient parchment. It was my last chance.' He became a half-dead monster, better known on the island as the eater of children.

Such a deed estranged him for all time from the rest of his countrymen, and, being a Fogelfree, he escaped through the Westgorge into the No-Man's-Land of Halunnsore. Here, he met with a mysterious race of people called the Yougoloths, worshipped by the lizardmen living in the north and south of No-Man's-Land. He passed through their Trial of Foreign Blood, and was permitted to settle on their land under the condition he would never leave again.

The Yougoloths, being a race of seers, imparted on Waldemar there would be a great heavenly strife amongst the Gods and that great blood will be spilt. Waldemar became convinced this is what the prophecy was referring to and, believed he could write himself into the prophecy by repelling the Orks.

Wishing to leave to fulfill his destiny, the Yougoloths prevented him. He then made a bargain with them. Being an ordained priest of the Gods (which he had never revealed to them) he knew how a magickal barrier separating the Yougoloths from the rest of the island could be lifted. If they made him a wealthy man and permitted to take a ship thence to Ashenwyrm, he would lift the barrier and permit them feast on the residents of the Island. And they did, and so he left his countrymen behind.


The islanders started slowly dying off. Their children would disappear, and their women would be barren and bear nearly no children. If they did conceive, miscarriege and stillbirth was extremely common, so that the population of the islanders dwindled. This is because Yougoloths feed on children (as Jarga did).


Following the events of Volume III, namely the killing of King Drachenwill, the High Divine Waldemar seizes opportunity to claim what he believes to be his destiny and launches an assault into the disordered Kingdom of Ashenwyrm to forge the Sun Empire. In doing so, his reinterpretation of the Elderlief begins to be forcibly spread throughout the North. Temples are being destroyed, priests of the old faith killed and replaced with the new.
