Bockenzander

Created Wednesday 11 May 2022

A dynasty tracing its roots to the year roughly 500. The first head of the house was Lord Anseld Bockenzænder who rose to prominence mainly as the chief vassal of the dishonoured House Fugue following the partion of the house's lands. He married Lady Griffild Rosswight who, without his knowing, was already growing full with his child, at their wedding-day.

At the eve of their wedding Lady Rosswight was violently abducted by Necromancer Galserow (distantly related to Valindra's Master). A great fighting broke out during which many of Galserow's followers were slain but he himself escaped with his prize.

Lord Anseld Bockenzander pursued the fleeing warlock but alas lost him in the thick Kingslandshire woods. Heartbroken and swearing vengeance, he set up a rescue-party and delegated his lordly duties to his half-brother. With his companions he searched the Northlands for three years before eventually finding Galserow's lair and slaying him therein.

Gods' withheld their mercy that night, however, as of his lover remained but bones. In the depths of the tombs he found a boy, three years of age. Thinking him to be the vile spawn of the Necromancer's defloration of his dead lover, Lord Anseld tore the boy's shirt, ready to pierce his chest through the heart with his sword.

To his great wonder, as he positioned himself to slay the crying child, he found a birthmark identical to his own on the boy's breast, realising he was his son.

The boy succeeded him in due time as the Lord of Crystalthorpe and the surrounding lands. Lord Anseld never remarried and never begot more children. After his death, at his own request, he was buried in a newly-built house-tomb next to his wife Lady Griffild Bockenzander.

The Tombstone reads:
>Here Lie at Peace
>Lord Anseld Bockenzander
>& His Loving Wife
>Lady Griffild Bockenzander
>Severed from her Lover on Wedding-day
>& Never Reunited
