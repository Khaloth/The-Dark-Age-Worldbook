Gainrock

Created Wednesday 25 August 2021

Gainrock is a massive mountain inverted on top of itself hovering above the Daemonblood Lagoon, 'Tegos Aimma Daei' in Celestial, situated on the Fallen Angel's Islet in the Far Noth near the Worldsedge. The Lagoon is connected subeterrenially with the open sea.

The Legend

According to the myth, during the archangel Zariel's fall into madness, one of her celestial captains stole her amulet, which she had worn into every battle with the daemons, and preserved within the amulet the last glimmers of purity left in her soul. He was deathly wounded during his theft and had only barely managed to flee into the Mortal Realm. He lay dying upon a small islet at the Edge of the World knowing Zariel would never find him. His act of selflessness and dedication pleased the Good Gods so much they raised the mountain whereupon the angel died in the likeness of Mount Celestia and buried him within with the amulet he had guarded.

First Mennish Contact

For thousands of years the islet lay idle at the World's End abandoned and undisturbed. In time, a group of renegate mermen settled near its shores and flooded and expanded Gainrock as their impenetrable keep against their betrayed brethren. During the Age of Heroes Men made their first contact with the islet and charted it on their maps, although they feared and misunderstood its enigmatic nature.

Cult of the Banished

Cult of the Banished, under the command of Master Nithad drove out the mermen and drained Gainrock to make use of its large waterworn halls. At the behest of their master, they made the upper part of the halls into a large labyrinth split into four storeys the first three testing the worshipper's might, wit, and devotion respectively; the prize lay on the fourth storey. They finalised their work with opening a minor gate into the Abyss. Thence regular mennish sacrifice commenced.

Conquest

During the Age of Conquest, the Cult of the Banished grew considerably in power, though not in number. It remained largely a very prestigious and exclusive organisation of daemonologists and daemonworshippers. It was during this time the outdriven mermen attempted to ally themselves with their scornful cousins, who haughtily refused them.

The mermen led a singular devastating and brutal attack on the cultists' haven. Having lain dormant to the cultists' eyes for centuries, they caught the landdwellers by surprise. The fighting ceased before dusk and by nightfall all the cultists either lay dead or had escaped into the upper floors where the mermen dared not follow. The mermen then sealed the entrance thither and let the escapees to starve.

Rediscovery

In 1346, Allastor Angelson rediscovered Gainrock and surpassed all three challenges claiming the long-lost malachite.
