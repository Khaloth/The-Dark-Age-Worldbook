Wondergarden

Created Sunday 3 January 2021

Entrance

Wondergarden is hidden behind an illusion-wall in the ruins of the birthplace of the founder of Roenloch the Great, the founder of Waterdhavian Academy of Arcane Arts. Two six-pointed stars of Mystra need to be touched concurrently by two distinct magic-users for a magical circuit to connect send all in range to the Ethereal Plane.

Governing

Wondergarden is led by a Tribune of the Eight Noble Mages who are responsible for the policy-making and major decision. To their aid they have created illusory several artificial servents although their number dwindles as the magick needed to repair them has been lost. These servants have no names and instead are called by their function: Cook, Usherer, Guardian, etc. They have been bound by a set of rules called the Protocol, which is, in a sense, all they know.

The Tribune is split into eight Highmoots:

  1. Elder-moot (Supreme control over other moots)
  2. Shield-moot (Defence) - Master Almarr
  3. Reckon-moot (Finances) - Master Quinn
  4. Witten-moot (Academy) - Master Henrick
  5. Fremming-moot (Outside relations) - Highmootman Raven Ashburn
  6. Stocking-moot (Internal management) - Master Almo
  7. Healer-moot (Medicine - Master Bonrad
  8. Grounds-moot (Physical upkeep and illusion upkeep) - Master Wollis

Law-enforcement

There are 12 magickally conjured and managed Usherers who share consciousness whose purpose is to enforce the laws of Wondergarden

Life in Wondergarden

Participation in the Wonderdarden's community is purely voluntary and all those who come are free to leave or return at any point. During their stay, however, they are expected to fulfill a function assigned to them the first time they had come.

Dead Man's Chapel

Dead Man's Chapel is the underground temple Roenloch the Great, then Roenloch the Lich-mage, had built himself so he might do penance against he crime against menhood. He would descend into the complete darkness for months or even years on end in incessant prayer. Following an incident of unknown origins, there was a great magickal explosion in which Roenloch was killed.

The Illusion is weak and weakening in the Dead Man's Chapel as very few men go there. It is used at the moment as a secondary dungeon complex for holding prisoners, although it is hardly ever used. Its usage is so scarce in fact that it had taken months for the establishing mages of Wondergarden to discover the place had been already inhabited by two opposing tribes of Dark Goblins (blue-skinned goblins, related to the Green Goblins in similar way as the duergar are to dwarves). By the time they did learn, the goblins had long adopted to the trickery of the Illusion and even learnt to use it against their tribal opponents. Although the riddance of the creatures had been on the agenda of the Eight Noble Mages since the discovery, it is a very low-priority effort.

The Dead Man's chapel is an unhospitable place, a home to various kinds of deadly fungi, dark goblins, and various malevolent spirits. The greatest point of contension is a large underground lake integrated into the chapel in the centre of which stands a lonely island upon which Roenloch the Great used to pray. The reason for the dispute is that it is the only source of drinkable water in the complex.

Agony

The Wondergarden is a major producer of Agony. They kidnap and torture young members of the order.
