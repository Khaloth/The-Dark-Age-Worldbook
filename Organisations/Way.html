Way

Created Thursday 08 April 2021

Acronym for Valindra, Vvishmaker, Aestra, and Yaga.

Present Condition

  • Running out of money after the mint had been destroyed
  • No new gatherers are being created after the destruction of their den
  • The outposts of the Way are exposed like they have not been since Men first arrived at the Elven Shores
  • Aboleths press Valindra to hasten; she fears them
  • Treason by Mother Jarga is feared and nearly expected

The Aims

The Way wishes to support Aboleths, the only living servants of the Eldergods in the World, in their efforts to pervert the deathcycle (cf. Creation Myth) and enthrone their masters for all time.

To definitively end the Cycle, the Aboleths have put together the following plan

  • Weaken the Gods
  • Slay Worldsbane
  • Free the Forgotten One

Justified by the following train of thought. If only one Eldergod is free, the quarrelsome nature of the Eldergods cannot cause an infight which would threaten the stability of their rule; next, if Worldsbane dies, there are no Ashes wherefrom new gods could be born of the Fathergods; finally if the Gods are significantly weakened themselves through infighting, the Forgotten One will have a good chance at defeating them.

In doing so, the Way seek to forestall the second death of men which comes with the world-burning by Worldsbane at the end of each deathcycle; thus their aim is a form of immortality.

The Plan

  • SETTING HISTORY FAVOURABLE
    • Race with a weak connexion to their Gods must become dominant
- Men have been outcast from their home by their own Gods. Their rulers, moreover, are quarrelsome and vengeful. They are perfect.
  • Their king must settle within reach of Aboleths influence
- The seat of the King of Ashenwyrm, the highest seat in the North for a long time, lies over a vast complex of underground flooded shafts over which the Elves had built sewers and underground shrines

  • SETTING THE SCENE
    • Weaken Men's Gods and Men's faith in them
- The Godswar has seen several Gods killed and trust among themselves shattered. Moreover, their negligence towards their believers during their infighting has left many Men resentful
  • Engulf the continent in a never-before-seen racial conflict
- Valindra has fostered relations with Laminus, the leader of the Sons of the Wyrmslayer. She is smuggling arms and wildfire into nearly every major city in the North through faux-imports of glass. To this end, she befriended House Bockenzander whom she pays enormous sums of money to enable her to smuggle the goods under their noble name.
Presently, following the destruction of the Way's mint, she is running low on resources and thus is forced to take drastic measures. See Chapter 5.
  • Enthrone a favourable ruler
- Prince Veligard is planned to succeed his father. He has been visited by the Witchqueens in his dreams since early childhood and is loyal to their cause.

THE BLOODY END

  • Persuade ruler to sacrifice 100,000 Men in connexion with the conflict
  • Summon an aspect of the Forgotten One; nurture him through futher sacrifice
  • Let the Forgotten One demonstrate his power by ending the conflict; steer Men away from their uncaring Gods
  • Through the Forgotten One, slay Snirrthingur (Worldsbane).
  • Persecute and kill the remaining adherents of Elderlief

They shall entrap Worldsbane while he slumbers and awaken him. They will tell the deathlingfolk the only thing standing between

To summon the aspect of the Forgotten One, a grand ritual is needed, channeling the dormant powers of the Urfoce. A million souls gathered during a single day. After they are free, persuading the petty-folk to abandon their old weak Gods shall be a childsplay. And with the last worshipper of the Old Ways converted or dead, so shall the New Gods slowly vanish into nothingness. The Witchqueens shall hold illimitable dominion over all.

The ultimate motivation of the Wayfarers is fear of Death; namely the fear of their second, inevitable, and ultimate death - the death of the soul. During the Devouring, not only is the physical world destroyed, but so are the souls of all those who had been born on it. This brings on their second and final death beyond which nothing lies.

This is the true meaning of Death Shall Die. The second, and much more important, death is tacitly understood. It is thus that the Way seeks to forestall the final death of all those who have ever been alive since the last cycle ended. They aim to achieve immortality for all men, alebeit a neverending life of servitude.

To prevent the next cycle, the Devourer himself must die. The Way believes the Elder Gods stand the best chance when the time comes to kill the Devourer of the World.

The Means

The Way has worked over thousands of years in the background, shaping the world and civilisation from the shadows; their efforts thwarted from time to time by the Iron Dawn.


The Gatherers

They worship Aestra fanatically. See the mennish soul and worship.

The Gatherer is begotten in a vile necromantic rite from a mennish body and a soul. It is a complex procedure developed and perfected by Aestra after her hundred years in Shadowdark. They excell at kidnapping all manners of creatures, including Men. They are especially apt at kidnapping small children and animals. The gatherers possess superhuman strength and nimbleness and are capable of basic deception tactics to lure their victims. They can also act collectively, although their communication within a group is rather limited.

They seem like mutilated men with terrific disfigurements scarring their cold bodies. They are not undead in the usual sense but they are not alive either. Unlike undead they require food and water for sustenance but unlike men they do not feel pain or have a conscience. Their usual diet comprises raw meat. Rarely ever do they drink water, for that purpose they drink the blood of the animal they eat.

They are usually dressed in large black robes, covering their monstrous bodies.
