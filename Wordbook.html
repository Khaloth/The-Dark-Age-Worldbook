Colloquial Terms

  1. Brown - adj. n. A colloquial term for Prodaysian
  2. Churl - n. [derogative potentially] A man of non-noble origin - a freeman or a serf
  3. Earg - [derogative] A womanly man. A coward. Origin: Old Norse 'ergi' - 'effeminate man'
  4. Earmarked - n. An elf
  5. Holmgang - n. [legal] A wager of battle either to the first blood or to death. Technically anyone can challenge anyone else to a holmgang. Holmgang usually takes place within three to seven days of the challenge on either a specified place or a place designated to that purpose generally. If the accused does not arrive, the accuser can instead swing his sword three times and finish with a stab which officially recognises him as the victor. If the accuser does not arrive, the accused is the shout nithing! three times and stab the air at the point of which, the accuser is considered a nithing.
  6. Holtganger - n. [legal] A man who has been named an outlaw for the rest of his life; a sentence usually delivered for the gravest offences
  7. Kingstead - n. [legal] The supreme legislative and judicial body in Ashenwyrm. They preside over the most high-profile cases and make, with the consent of the king, laws governing the kingdom
  8. Prodaysian - adj. n. A person descended of or associated with the original elvish Traitor Lords; see Traitor Lord. Colloquial: Brown. Origin: Greek 'προδοσία' - 'treachery'
  9. Serpent - adj. n. Someone born under the constellation of the Serpent; a potential magick-user
  10. Serpenthood - n. The condition of being born under the constellation of the Serpent; magickal potential
  11. Strangler - n. A despicable death-deserving man. A nithing.
  12. Thing(stead) - n. [legal] A regional ruling body overseeing one of the shires of Ashenwyrm or other nothern kingdom. A thingstead operates at a higher level than a manorial court and has the legal power to override a lord's ruling, though this practice is discouraged.
  13. Traitor Lord - n. A member of old elvish nobility who joined the cause of the First Men and helped them establish dominance over the western part of the continent in exchange for lands
  14. Werheim - n. 1) The legendary home of Men, 2.1) Something wonderful, That would be a Werheim. 2.2) Something tantalising, The Werheim of the academia
  15. Wylderman -n. [legal] A man who has been named an outlaw for a specified period; usually three years.

Religious Terms


Elderlief

  1. Algard - n. The common-grounds realm of the Gods. Since Godswar, a conflict in which it became thoroughly drenched in the essence of blood and death, it became an extension of Myrkul's realm.
  2. Elderliever - n. A follower of Elderlief
  3. Fathergods - n. The previous generation of gods of whose blood the present gods were born.
  4. Helgenfell - n. The mountain upon which sits Helgenfellgard, the City of Judgement, where the soul of dead men travels upon death to be judged by the Ysterhild, the Tribunal of the Seven Judges
  5. Helgenfellgard - n. The City of Judgement whither the souls of the dying travel upon death.
  6. Nithe - n. One of the fixed set of unforgivable misdeeds whose commitment meant immediate banishment and dehumanisation
  7. Nithestand - n. A large wooden pole usually with a face of a man carven into the surface and a head of a horse or other valuable livestock-animal impaled upon it. It is used to curse nithings.
  8. Nithing - n. A man who had committed one of the Nithes condemned to eternity in the Nine Hells and placed outside the law.
  9. Snirrthingur - n. Also called Worldsbane. The Serpent which devours the world at the end of each cycle.
  10. Urfolk - n. The original races saved at the end of each (universe) cycle. The urfolk are: Men, Elves, Dwarves, Orks, Mermen. See Way for more details.
  11. Verdentrow - n. The Tree of the World initially created through the merging of the Root of Bane and the Root of Flame. It is planted in commemoration of the defeat of the Eldergods. Also called Worldtree.
  12. Werheim - n. The primordial home of Men, see also Werheim in colloquial use above
  13. Ysterhild - n. The tribunal which assesses the dead individual's worth in life after death and subsequently lets one of the seven seating members to choose to either permit the soul into their respective realm in the Seven Heavens or deny the deceased access to all of them thereby dooming him to eternity in the Nine Hells. A common saying goes The Eight Judge welcomes all.
